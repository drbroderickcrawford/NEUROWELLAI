# GLDS-766 LAR Target-Shift Robustness Checks

## Summary

Three robustness checks evaluated whether the observed upward shift among targets of flight-downregulated miRNAs specifically supports relaxation of miRNA-mediated repression.

The results preserve a strong theme-specific neurobehavioral signal, but they do not provide strong evidence that miRNA downregulation is the specific cause of the mRNA shift.

## 1. Negative Control: Random miRNA Target Sets

The observed functional validated target set contained 121 genes represented in the processed brain mRNA table. For 10,000 permutations, 50 random measured miRNAs were selected, their functional validated targets were identified, and each random target set was downsampled to exactly 121 genes.

| Metric | Observed targets | Random-miRNA mean | Empirical p |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Mean mRNA log2FC | 1.675 | 1.551 | 0.126 |
| Median mRNA log2FC | 1.943 | 1.909 | 0.228 |
| Proportion higher in flight | 84.3% | 81.8% | 0.192 |

The observed validated-target shift was not significantly stronger than shifts generated by random miRNA target sets of the same gene-set size.

## 2. Opposite-Direction Test

Targets of the top 50 flight-upregulated miRNAs were tested for a downward mRNA shift.

| Metric | Result |
|---|---:|
| Functional validated targets represented | 92 |
| Median mRNA log2FC | 1.947 |
| Mean mRNA log2FC | 1.315 |
| Proportion lower in flight | 21.7% |
| One-sided Wilcoxon p for downward shift | 0.983 |
| Random-gene mean-shift p | 0.924 |

Targets of flight-upregulated miRNAs did not shift downward. This does not support a bidirectional miRNA-repression signature.

## 3. Theme-Specific Robustness

| Theme | Genes | Median log2FC | Higher in flight | FDR versus all mRNA | Permutation FDR versus other validated targets |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Neuroplasticity | 57 | 1.994 | 93.0% | 0.00038 | 0.00060 |
| Synaptic signaling | 46 | 2.014 | 95.7% | 0.00038 | 0.00140 |
| Cognitive function | 19 | 2.033 | 100% | 0.00386 | 0.00060 |
| Neuroinflammation | 26 | 2.102 | 84.6% | 0.00386 | 0.01635 |
| Stress response | 26 | 1.947 | 84.6% | 0.01456 | 0.02628 |
| Circadian biology | 5 | 2.446 | 100% | 0.02390 | 0.09759 |

Plasticity, synaptic signaling, cognition, immune regulation, and stress themes remained stronger than randomly sampled sets of other validated target genes. Circadian biology remained stronger than the overall mRNA background but did not outperform other validated targets after correction.

## Interpretation

The robustness checks do not support a specific genome-wide model in which flight-downregulated miRNAs directly cause broad target-mRNA derepression. Random miRNA target sets produced similarly strong upward shifts, and targets of flight-upregulated miRNAs did not shift downward.

However, genes linked to neuroplasticity, synaptic signaling, cognition, stress response, and immune regulation are disproportionately shifted upward within the validated target-gene space.

This pattern may reflect coordinated neurobehavioral adaptation, shared upstream regulation, database structure, or a combination of these mechanisms. It should not currently be described as confirmed relaxation of miRNA-mediated repression.
